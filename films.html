EARLY AFRICAN AMERICAN FILM WEBSITE REDESIGN

RECONSTRUCTING THE HISTORY OF SILENT RACE FILMS

Early African American Film is a digital humanities capstone project that provide a platform for the purpose of raising awareness, promoting research, and highlighting the significance of early African American race film in film history. My team of eight other undergraduate students were tasked with redesigning the experience of a previous digital humanities project and better showcase its uses and achievements.The original site received a significant amount of press and traffic, and we believed revamping the site could help boost this topic to a greater extent.

In order to do so, we conducted stakeholder interviews, user interviews, and google surveys to gather the sufficient research needed to suggest site improvements. I was responsible for interviewing stakeholders and leading the web design process. We completed the project over the course of 10 weeks and presented our project to the program directors at the end of the spring quarter.

Role: Designer, Developer
Tools: Github, Mobirise, Photoshop
Completed: June 2018
Client: UCLA Digital Humanities Program
